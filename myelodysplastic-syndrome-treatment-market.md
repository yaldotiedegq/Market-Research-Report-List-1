<p><p>The Myelodysplastic Syndrome (MDS) treatment market features two main types of stem cell transplantation: allogeneic and autologous. Allogeneic stem cell transplantation involves the infusion of stem cells from a healthy donor, offering the potential for a complete cure and a new immune system, but carries risks of graft-versus-host disease. In contrast, autologous stem cell transplantation uses the patient's own stem cells, minimizing rejection risks, but may not be as effective against underlying disease. Both approaches aim to restore normal blood cell production.</p></p>
