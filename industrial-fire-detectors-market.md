Heat Detectors sense temperature increases, making them ideal for environments with dust or steam. Ionization Detectors react rapidly to flaming fires, while Photoelectric Detectors are better for smoldering fires, utilizing light scattering. Ionization/Photoelectric Detectors combine both technologies for a broader response. Other types may include flame detectors and gas detectors, enhancing safety in industrial settings where various fire risks exist.</p></p>
