<p><p>Crossflow membrane filtration systems encompass various types, including microfiltration and ultrafiltration, nanofiltration, and reverse osmosis, along with other specialized methods. Microfiltration and ultrafiltration primarily remove larger particles and macromolecules, making them suitable for biological and pharmaceutical applications. Nanofiltration serves to separate divalent ions and smaller molecules, often used in water softening. Reverse osmosis effectively purges contaminants at the molecular level, ideal for drinking water purification. Other methods include specialized applications tailored to specific separation needs across industries.</p></p>
