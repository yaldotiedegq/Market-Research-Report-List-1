<p><p>Positive and negative photoresist removers are chemicals used in the semiconductor and electronics industries to remove photoresist layers after the lithography process. Positive photoresists become soluble when exposed to light, allowing the unexposed areas to be washed away. Conversely, negative photoresists harden upon exposure, making it necessary to remove the unexposed areas. Both types of removers play a critical role in the fabrication processes of microprocessors, printed circuit boards, and various electronic devices.</p><p>The Positive and Negative Photoresist Remover Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.7% during the forecast period. <p><p>The photoresist remover market is divided into two main types: positive and negative photoresist removers. Positive photoresist removers are designed to dissolve the areas where the photoresist was exposed to light, allowing for the underlying substrate to be revealed. In contrast, negative photoresist removers target the areas that were not exposed to light, removing the unexposed regions while leaving the hardened, exposed pattern intact. Each type serves specific applications in semiconductor manufacturing and microfabrication processes.</p></p>
