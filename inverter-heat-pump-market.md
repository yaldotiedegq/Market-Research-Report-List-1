<p><p>The inverter heat pump market includes two main types: ducted and ductless systems. Ducted inverter heat pumps distribute heated or cooled air through a network of ducts, providing uniform temperature control across multiple rooms. They are ideal for larger homes and offer a concealed installation. In contrast, ductless inverter heat pumps, also known as mini-split systems, consist of indoor units mounted on walls with no ductwork required, allowing for flexible placement and efficient zone heating and cooling in smaller spaces.</p></p>
